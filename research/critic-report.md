## 2. Counter-evidence on edge candidates

### EC-1 (KXHIGH weather maker calibration)

- **The Zerve study cited as the 14.8x edge is calibration on settled prices, not a live trading study.** It does not document out-of-sample partition. There is no claim of fill rate, no slippage model, no maker-vs-taker decomposition. The synthesis treats a calibration paper as a profitability proof. That conflation is the single biggest analytical weakness in the document.
- **Northlake Labs (above) describes the latency arms race as "pros react within seconds of every NWS cycle."** EC-1 says "cancel on NWS cycle update" - but if the bot's reaction is slower than the arb fleet (which it almost certainly is from a residential Windows + WSL2 host in Washington state), the bot's resting orders get filled AT the stale price after NWS has already updated, which is the textbook adverse-selection failure mode.
- **suislanchez/polymarket-kalshi-weather-bot is a public open-source bot doing approximately what EC-1 proposes** (KXHIGH + GFS 31-member ensemble, Kelly sizing). Their stated PEAK profit is $1.8k, with no mention of the drawdown or trailing distribution. If a public bot is already on this strategy, the residual edge is being shared. A new entrant from WA on $50 cannot expect to outcompete a live bot whose author has already iterated.
- **Bartlett & O'Hara (2026, n=41.6M trades) find makers profitable but via a YES-overbet behavioral surplus on NO-settling markets.** Daily-high temperature markets are NOT obvious YES-bias markets (no ideological pull toward warm or cold). The maker-profit mechanism that supports the population statistic (makers -10% per trade is a gross figure, but Bartlett shows positive net in single-name markets after the surplus) may not transfer to KXHIGH.
- **Counterparty analysis is missing from the synthesis.** Who is on the other side of the EC-1 maker quote? In weather markets the counterparty is overwhelmingly other bots and arb desks (Northlake confirms this). That is the worst possible counterparty composition: there is no dumb money on the other side to subsidize the maker. A maker quote in a bot-vs-bot market is a Cournot duopoly, not an edge harvest.

**Critic assessment of EC-1: the gross-edge claim (4-7pp) is calibration-derived and likely overstated by an order of magnitude in live execution. After fees (0.88% round-trip), latency disadvantage (residential vs co-lo), and adverse selection from a bot-dominated counterparty pool, the realistic net edge is in [-1pp, +1pp]. Mode is small negative.**

### EC-2 (politics underconfidence)

- Bürgi/Deng/Whelan 2025 explicitly note FLB is "weakening in 2025 data." Becker (2026) notes maker-taker gap was 1.02pp on politics, much smaller than on entertainment (4.79pp). Politics is contested by sharp syndicates.
- 2025 was not a federal election year and 2026 mid-terms are months out. Non-election-year politics volume on Kalshi is low. EC-2 is described as "episodic" by the synthesis itself; in concrete terms, it produces fills for maybe 6 weeks per year and the rest of the time generates no trades.
- The Delta=0.53 widening-by-trade-size finding from arXiv 2602.19520 was measured during a high-volume election period. There is no published evidence it persists at the lower volumes seen in 2025-2026 non-cycle politics markets.

**Critic assessment of EC-2: opportunistic at best, not a primary thesis. Agree with synthesis on framing. Net edge plausible only around discrete events with paper-trail polling deviance.**

### Edges that are dead

EC-3 (cross-platform arb), EC-4 (BTC lat-arb), EC-5 (entertainment): all correctly killed in the briefs. No counter-evidence found that resurrects any of them at the $100 retail scale.

## 3. Weak assumptions in the synthesis

1. **The -$30 to +$15 outcome range is not derived; it is vibes.** No Monte Carlo, no fee model, no fill-rate assumption, no holding-period distribution backs that interval. It appears to be a hand-wave consistent with "small loss, small win." If the bot fails to fill or fills only adversely, the realistic floor is closer to -$50 (the cap) than -$30, because fees + slippage on illiquid contracts can chew through a bankroll faster than the brief implies. **Recommend the synthesis either show the math or remove the interval.**
2. **"Maker fee 4x more forgiving" framing is too optimistic.** It assumes the maker FILL happens at the quoted price. But maker fills are conditional on someone hitting the order; the hitter is usually informed (per Bartlett-O'Hara VPIN). The 4x advantage assumes uniform fill probability, which is wrong by construction. A correctly framed comparison: maker net edge = (gross_edge - 0.88%) * fill_rate * (1 - adverse_selection_rate). With fill rate ~30-50% and adverse selection ~50-70% on the filled subset, the maker fee advantage compresses substantially.
3. **The synthesis under-weights tail risk in WA.** The AG IS seeking a preliminary injunction; it is pled in the complaint, not unscheduled (synthesis is factually wrong here). If granted with 30-day comply-or-cease language, an open position from 2026-05 could be force-settled or held in Kalshi escrow pending civil resolution. The "flatten cleanly and stop" plan assumes Kalshi continues normal withdrawal mechanics during a WA-specific injunction event; that is an assumption, not a guarantee. The WA AG is using the Recovery of Money Lost at Gambling Act, which suggests the state views WA traders as victims rather than complicit - which is favorable - but the practical effect on a live account during an injunction window is unspecified.
4. **The 9th Circuit oral argument signal is omitted.** The panel "appeared to lean Nevada's way" per the Nevada Current (2026-04-16). The synthesis lists "opinion pending" but does not reflect the directional read of the argument. If the 9th rules for Nevada (likely per the panel's questioning), WA is directly affected because WA sits in the 9th Circuit. This shortens the access-loss window from "3-12 months" to plausibly "1-6 months."
5. **The fee tractability claim treats $0.50 strikes as the reference.** KXHIGH shoulder strikes are deeper into 15-40c range per the brief. Fee at 30c is ceil(7 * 0.30 * 0.70) = ceil(1.47) = 2c per taker side, or about 0.5c per maker side. That's slightly better for makers (lower fee) but means the maker advantage relative to gross edge shrinks because the gross edge is also smaller on deep strikes.
6. **Sports is 89% of Kalshi REVENUE in 2025, not 72% of volume.** Source: ingame.com / Sportico / Sacra. The Agent B figure (72%) is on the low side and misframes the regulatory exposure: the WA AG suit is squarely targeting Kalshi's main business. If Kalshi loses sports nationally (plausible after a 9th Circuit loss + SCOTUS denial), the platform's economics shift and so does its appetite to keep marginal-revenue WA-resident retail accounts open. That is an additional access-loss vector the synthesis misses.
7. **The "EC-1 is the one credible edge" framing rests on a single unvalidated study (Zerve CalibShi).** That is single-source dependence in the most consequential conclusion in the document. There is no independent replication of the 14.8x ECE figure.
8. **The synthesis does not estimate the probability of preliminary-injunction grant.** Maryland already DENIED a preliminary injunction TO Kalshi in a parallel case; the WA AG is seeking one AGAINST Kalshi. These are different burdens. But the bench of state-court judges in WA who view sports event contracts as gambling is the wrong audience for Kalshi's CFTC-preemption argument. A 30-50% probability of preliminary injunction in the next 6-12 months is the honest range. The synthesis doesn't put a number on it.

## 4. Required additional research before Phase 2

Things that, if wrong, change the proceed/kill recommendation. Not nice-to-haves.

1. **Live fill-rate test on KXHIGH maker quotes.** Spend $5-10 on a one-week resting-order test on KXHIGHNY shoulder strikes, log every quote-to-fill latency, every fill price vs subsequent NWS update, every adverse-selection event (filled then market moved against you within 5 minutes). Target sample: 50 fills. Without this number the EC-1 edge claim is not falsifiable. If maker fill rate is below ~40% or adverse-selection rate is above ~60%, kill EC-1.
2. **Direct download of the Kalshi Fee Schedule PDF.** The synthesis confirms via secondary sources but operator should pull the live PDF from a clean session before live trading. Specifically verify: (a) standard formula, (b) any KXHIGH-specific schedule, (c) any "special event" fee table differing from the 7% standard.
3. **Lychee L2 dataset pricing quote.** "Contact sales" is not a budget. If Lychee is $50/month or less, EC-1 backtest is properly fundable from the $100 cap. If it's $500/month or more, EC-1 backtest is materially impaired and the synthesis's "doable but engineering-intensive" claim becomes "not doable on this budget."
4. **WA-licensed attorney review of restitution exposure.** The Recovery of Money Lost at Gambling Act allows WA consumers to reclaim gambling losses, which favors the operator if they LOSE money. But Consumer Protection Act counts allow restitution AND disgorgement against Kalshi. The risk is that if Kalshi loses or settles, all WA-resident accounts may be force-closed and balances returned to depositors (good outcome for the operator) - but also potentially held in escheatment for months (bad outcome). A 30-minute consultation with a WA attorney familiar with consumer-finance regulatory actions is required. Budget separately.
5. **Confirmation that Kalshi separates `read` / `trade` / `withdraw` scopes per API key.** Search results indicate this is supported (Dec 2025 scopes field). Synthesis flagged this as Open Question #9; it appears resolved. Operator should verify in the live dashboard and confirm trade-only key generation works without enabling withdraw.
6. **Out-of-sample validation of Zerve isotonic recalibration.** Pull 500-1000 settled KXHIGHNY contracts from a held-out window, fit isotonic on the first half, score the second half, compute realized edge vs raw market. If the 14.8x ECE figure does NOT survive a clean train/test split, EC-1 is dead. This is the highest-value research item on the list.
7. **Counterparty composition on KXHIGH.** Is there any retail flow at all? Bartlett-O'Hara provides the framework (VPIN, Glosten-Harris) but does not break out by series. Without a non-trivial retail presence, EC-1 is bot vs bot and the edge is competed away. Operator can estimate this by sampling 50 fills on KXHIGHNY across a week and asking: do fills cluster around NWS update times (bot pattern) or are they uniformly distributed across the day (retail pattern)?
8. **WSocket subscription cap and reconnect behavior on demo.** Synthesis flags this as unknown. Measure empirically before relying on WS for both market data and order updates. If subscription cap is below 50, the 25-50-resting-orders proposal in the synthesis is infeasible without multiple connections.

## 5. Critic's verdict

**KILL, or at most a very narrow CONDITIONAL PROCEED gated on item 6 above (out-of-sample Zerve replication).**

I disagree with the synthesis's "conditional proceed with $25 tuition budget" framing. Three reasons.

First, **the EC-1 edge claim rests on a single unvalidated study with no out-of-sample test.** That is the most fragile possible foundation. Until item 6 in the missing-research list is done and produces a positive result, EC-1 is not a candidate, it is a hypothesis. The synthesis should not be greenlighting Phase 2 on a hypothesis.

Second, **the access risk is materially worse than the synthesis reflects.** A preliminary injunction motion IS on file in WA (not "unscheduled"). The 9th Circuit panel leaned Nevada's way at oral argument. Kalshi's main revenue stream (89% sports) is squarely in the regulator's crosshairs. The probability that WA access closes within 6 months is in the 30-50% range, not "3-12 months hand-wave." A bot launched in June 2026 may have 8-16 weeks of live runtime, which is below the synthesis's own 200-fill go/no-go gate threshold. The math implies the gate never trips before access closes.

Third, **even granting the synthesis's framing, the expected loss is more likely closer to the cap than the synthesis's -$30 floor.** The -$30 floor assumes the bot fills, executes cleanly, and just loses small amounts on fees and noise. The realistic failure mode is: 30 days of no-fill (or worse, adversely-selected fills only), followed by access closure mid-position, followed by withdrawal delay. In that scenario the operator loses time, may lose access to deposited funds for a quarter, and gains no learnings worth $25-30.

**The honest framing: this is a project with strong learning value (engineering practice on real-money APIs, RSA-PSS signing, idempotent orders, reconciliation loops) and near-zero EV. Those learnings can be obtained for FREE on Kalshi's demo environment plus a $0 paper-trade-on-prod layer.** No live capital is needed to learn the engineering. If the operator wants the engineering learnings, build to demo and stop. If the operator wants the trading research, do item 6 (Zerve out-of-sample replication) BEFORE Phase 2 - it is a 1-2 day project on free data and it answers the central edge question. If it passes, then conditional-proceed becomes defensible.

**Bottom line: kill live trading. Run item 6 as a Phase 1.5 gate. If Zerve replicates out-of-sample at >5x ECE improvement on a clean partition, revisit. Otherwise this is sunk cost in waiting.**
